Learn Constitution

Constitution, Made Simple

Start with the most important Articles in everyday life and use them as your basic legal foundation.

Article 14

Equality Before Law

Every person must be treated equally before the law. The State cannot act unfairly or arbitrarily.

Use case: unfair treatment by a public authority or unequal rules for the same issue.
Article 19

Freedoms of Citizens

This includes speech, movement, association, and profession, subject to lawful restrictions.

Use case: speech, protests, social media expression, or restrictions on movement.
Article 21

Life and Personal Liberty

No one can be deprived of life or personal liberty except according to law. Courts read dignity and privacy into this right.

Use case: unlawful detention, abuse, privacy issues, or dignity-related violations.
Article 21A

Right to Education

Children between 6 and 14 years have the right to free and compulsory education.

Use case: school access, denial of admission, or child education rights.
Article 32

Right to Constitutional Remedies

If a fundamental right is violated, a person can approach the Supreme Court for relief.

Use case: strong constitutional protection when rights are directly violated.
Article 39A

Free Legal Aid

The State should ensure equal justice and provide free legal aid to those who cannot afford it.

Use case: legal help for weaker sections, women, workers, and vulnerable citizens.
